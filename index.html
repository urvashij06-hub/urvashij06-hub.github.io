About Me

I am a PhD research scholar in the Department of Applied Mathematics and Scientific Computing at the Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee, working at the intersection of nonlinear partial differential equations, symmetry methods and modern data-driven techniques. My research explores how mathematical structures such as Lie symmetries, conservation laws and self-similar solutions can be systematically used to analyse and simplify complex physical and biological systems, including traffic flow and population dynamics models.

Alongside analytical and semi-analytical approaches, I am actively interested in integrating machine learning and physics-informed neural networks with classical PDE modelling to develop reliable and interpretable computational frameworks for complex dynamical systems. My long-term goal is to bridge rigorous mathematical analysis with emerging AI-based tools for next-generation scientific computing.

Research Areas

  • Nonlinear partial differential equations
  • Lie symmetry analysis and invariant solutions
  • Conservation laws
  • Exact and solitonic solutions
  • Numerical simulation of PDEs
